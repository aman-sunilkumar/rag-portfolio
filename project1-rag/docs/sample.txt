Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning: A Primer

Artificial intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence
processes by computer systems, including learning, reasoning, and self-correction.

Machine learning (ML) is a subset of AI that enables systems to learn and improve
from experience without being explicitly programmed. ML focuses on developing
programs that access data and learn from it autonomously.

Deep learning is a subset of ML that uses neural networks with many layers to
analyze complex patterns. It has revolutionized fields like computer vision and
natural language processing.

Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) combines retrieval-based and generative AI.
When a question is asked, relevant documents are first retrieved from a knowledge
base, and then an LLM uses both the question and retrieved documents to generate
an accurate, grounded, and cited answer.

Large Language Models (LLMs) are AI systems trained on vast amounts of text data.
Examples include GPT-4o, Claude, and Llama. They perform tasks like summarization,
translation, and question answering.

Vector embeddings are numerical representations of text that capture semantic
meaning. Similar texts have embeddings close together in vector space, enabling
semantic search that goes beyond simple keyword matching.

ChromaDB is an open-source vector database that stores and retrieves embeddings
efficiently. It is commonly used in RAG pipelines for its simplicity and speed.

The transformer architecture, introduced in the paper "Attention is All You Need"
in 2017, is the foundation of modern LLMs. It uses self-attention mechanisms to
process sequences in parallel, enabling much faster training than prior approaches.

BM25 is a classical keyword-based retrieval algorithm. It scores documents based
on term frequency and inverse document frequency. Combining BM25 with vector
search (hybrid retrieval) often outperforms either method alone.
